# Lecture 14: Evolution of Communication

## Core Shift: Language for Coordination

> "The natural habitat of language is interaction" — Schegloff, 1996

Language is NOT primarily a message-sending system. It's a **coordinating system** that emerges from dynamical processes of communication in interaction.

> "Human behavior is a developmental product of a wider system than the system of human individual functions. It is a product of a system of social connections and social relations." — Vygotsky, 1930

## Experimental Semiotics

Studying communication as **joint action** — observe emergence of communication systems in laboratory settings.

## Example: Garrod et al., 2007 — "Pictionary" in the Lab

### Design
Players communicate easily confusable concepts by drawing:
- **SD−F:** 1 drawer, no feedback (imaginary receiver)
- **SD+F:** 1 drawer, with feedback (receiver also draws)
- **DD+F:** 2 drawers alternate, with feedback

### Results
- **SD+F:** Towards conventional iconicity (simplified but still recognizable)
- **DD+F:** Towards **abstractness** (further from original form)
- Alternating roles → greater simplification
- Re-using elements → alignment → quicker convergence
- Online interaction > step-by-step (email-like) → faster simplification

### Key Finding: Emergence of Arbitrariness
- Signs start iconic (resemble referent)
- Through interaction → become arbitrary and conventional
- The "burden of information-carrying shifts from individual signs into history"
- **Interaction history** is what creates conventionality

## Example: Galantucci, 2005 — Signs in Action

### Design
- Players must find each other in a game map (rooms)
- Points for meeting; penalties for time spent
- Can communicate graphically BUT medium prevents common symbols (distorts drawings)
- Natural language blocked

### Communication Systems That Emerged
- (a) Systems based on **numeration**
- (b) Systems based on **icons in rooms**
- (c) Systems based on **map layout**

### Key Insight
Communication emerges for **coordination of action** (not just reference/description). The goal is not "tell what you see" but "coordinate behavior."

## Features Dependent on Interaction History

### Arbitrariness
- Starts iconic → becomes arbitrary through repeated use
- Specific forms depend on initial "first use" (historical contingency)
- Convention = shared history, not inherent form-meaning link

### Complexity of Signs
- Complex environments → more structured signals
- "Learning by using" — signals that predict partner's behavior are retained
- Naming often happens AFTER coordination achieved (not before)

## Can Different Meanings Coexist?

**Yes** — an element can function well even with different internal meanings for each participant, as long as:
- It enables sufficient **coordination**
- It helps each participant **predict the other's behavior**
- Extreme divergence → communication breakdown

## Sources of Language Structuring

1. **Interaction & coordination needs** — shared goals drive conventions
2. **Cognitive constraints** — learnability, memorability, generalizability
3. **Communication pressures** — expressiveness, ambiguity resolution
4. **Cultural evolution** — iterated learning selects for transmissible structures
5. **Historical contingency** — early choices constrain later development

### Why Languages Aren't Simple
- Expressiveness demands (nuance, novelty)
- Compositionality more learnable than holistic signals
- Robustness to noise (redundancy)
- Historical contingency → structural persistence
- Multiple interacting forces at different timescales

## Why Difficult to Study Language Emergence

1. **Timescale** — thousands/millions of years
2. **Multiple factors** — biological, cognitive, social, cultural entangled
3. **No direct observation** — earliest stages lost
4. **Limited experimental control** — ethical/practical constraints
5. **Defining emergence** — when does signaling become "language"?
6. **Snowball effect** — once established, can't restart

## Lewis Signaling Game

### The Model
- Sender observes state of world
- Sender emits signal
- Receiver takes action based on signal
- Both rewarded if action matches state

### How Conventions Emerge
- Through evolutionary dynamics (reinforcement)
- No social reasoning needed — purely evolutionary
- Stable conventions emerge = **signaling system** (= Nash equilibrium)
- "Conventional meanings can emerge between plants and animals"

### Linguistic Meanings as Conventional
- Arbitrary connection between form and meaning
- Established through social agreement and shared usage
- Can shift due to: social change, language contact, prestige, ambiguity, internal processes

### Limitations of Lewis Game
1. Small discrete sets (oversimplified)
2. No compositionality
3. No syntax/grammar
4. Perfectly rational agents with complete info
5. No noise/errors
6. Objects assumed equally important
7. Fixed vocabulary

### Extensions
- Add syntax rules for combining signals
- Hierarchical structure
- Vocabulary constraints
- Allow structured/compositional signals
- → Can study emergence of grammar-like properties
